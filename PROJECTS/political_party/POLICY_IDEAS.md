## Policy Categories

Based on our principles and projects, policies could focus on:

---

## 1. FOOD & AGRICULTURE

### Urban Food Production
- **Right to grow** — Every citizen has right to grow food on unused public land
- **Seed sovereignty** — Right to save, exchange, and sell seeds freely
- **Urban agriculture zones** — Designated areas in cities for community food production
- **Rooftop/balcony growing incentives** — Tax benefits for food-producing spaces

### Water Conservation
- **Olla irrigation subsidies** — Support for water-efficient irrigation in gardens/farms
- **Rainwater harvesting** — Required in new buildings, encouraged in existing
- **Water rights reform** — Water as common good, not commodity

### Soil Health
- **Soil protection laws** — Prevent degradation, require regeneration
- **Composting infrastructure** — Municipal composting available to all
- **Biochar incentives** — Support for carbon-sequestering soil amendments

---

## 2. HOUSING & LAND

### Land Access
- **Community land trusts** — Support for community-owned land
- **Unused land activation** — Penalties for land banking, incentives for productive use
- **Garden plots for all** — Every neighborhood has community garden access

### Housing
- **Housing as right** — Not speculative commodity
- **Co-housing support** — Legal and financial frameworks for intentional communities
- **Green building standards** — Regenerative materials, energy positive

### Regenerative Development
- **Development must regenerate** — New projects must improve the land
- **Green space requirements** — Minimum % per development
- **Food forest integration** — Public spaces planted with edible landscapes

---

## 3. ENVIRONMENT & REGENERATION

### Desertification/Degradation
- **Land restoration fund** — Public money for restoring degraded land
- **Regeneration targets** — Measurable goals for soil improvement
- **Technique support** — Training and resources for restoration methods

### Biodiversity
- **Native plant requirements** — Public spaces prioritize native species
- **Pollinator corridors** — Connected habitat through urban/rural areas
- **Rewilding zones** — Areas allowed to self-regenerate

### Climate
- **Carbon farming payments** — Farmers paid for sequestering carbon
- **Regenerative agriculture transition** — Support moving away from degenerative practices
- **Local food systems** — Reduce food miles, support local producers

---

## 4. COMMUNITY & ECONOMY

### Sharing Economy (Real)
- **Tool libraries** — Public tool lending in every neighborhood
- **Skill sharing platforms** — Municipal support for skill exchange
- **Time banking** — Recognize and reward community contribution

### Local Economy
- **Local currency support** — Legal framework for community currencies
- **Small business protection** — Resist monopolies, support local
- **Cooperative support** — Legal and financial help for cooperatives

### Community Power
- **Participatory budgeting** — Communities decide local spending
- **Citizen assemblies** — Regular input on policy decisions
- **Delegated democracy** — Vote delegation on topics you care about

---

## 5. HEALTH & WELLBEING

### Mental Health
- **Green prescriptions** — Doctors can prescribe nature/gardening time
- **Therapeutic horticulture** — Funded as mental health intervention
- **Mushroom therapy** — Support psychedelic therapy research and access (Netherlands already progressive here)

### Community Health
- **Third spaces** — Fund community gathering places
- **Social connection** — Address loneliness as public health issue
- **Prevention focus** — Invest in keeping people healthy

### Food as Medicine
- **Prescription produce** — Fresh food available through healthcare
- **Nutrition education** — Schools, communities, healthcare
- **Food security** — No one goes hungry in Netherlands

## 6. TECHNOLOGY & DIGITAL

### Digital Rights
- **Data sovereignty** — Citizens own their data
- **Platform cooperatives** — Support alternatives to big tech
- **Open source preference** — Public money = open code

### AI Ethics
- **AI transparency** — Know when AI is making decisions
- **Human override** — Always a human in the loop
- **AI for common good** — Public AI infrastructure

### Digital Infrastructure
- **Municipal internet** — Community-owned connectivity
- **Digital literacy** — Everyone can participate
- **Accessibility** — Technology works for all abilities

---

## 7. GOVERNANCE & DEMOCRACY

### Transparency
- **Open by default** — All government data public unless sensitive
- **Decision documentation** — Every decision traceable
- **Citizen oversight** — Right to review government processes

### Participation
- **Lower voting age** — 16 for local elections
- **Easier candidacy** — Lower barriers to run for office
- **Recall mechanisms** — Hold representatives accountable

### Decentralization
- **Municipal power** — More decisions at local level
- **Regional autonomy** — Provinces have real authority
- **Community councils** — Neighborhood-level decision making

## 8. EDUCATION

### Life Skills
- **Growing food** — Every student learns to grow plants
- **Financial literacy** — Budgeting, saving, investing
- **Critical thinking** — Media literacy, logical reasoning

### Nature Connection
- **Outdoor education** — Regular time in nature
- **School gardens** — Every school has a garden
- **Sustainability curriculum** — Climate, ecology, regeneration

### Alternative Paths
- **Apprenticeships** — Valued equally to university
- **Lifelong learning** — Education doesn't end at graduation
- **Self-directed learning** — Support alternative education
