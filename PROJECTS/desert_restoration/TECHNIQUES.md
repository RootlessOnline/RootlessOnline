# Desert Restoration — Technique Library

> Detailed documentation of all desert restoration techniques.

## Overview

This document compiles proven techniques for desert soil restoration, with emphasis on the integrated approach combining water capture with subsurface irrigation.

---

## Core Principle

**Water is the limiting factor.**

All desert restoration techniques ultimately address one or more of:
1. Capturing more water
2. Storing water longer
3. Reducing water loss
4. Using water more efficiently

## 1. Water Capture Techniques

### 1.1 Zaï Pits

**Origin:** Traditional technique from Burkina Faso, Sahel region

**Description:**
Small pits (20-40 cm diameter, 10-20 cm deep) dug into hard, crusted soil to capture rainfall and concentrate nutrients.

**How It Works:**
- Breaks soil crust, improves infiltration
- Captures runoff that would otherwise flow away
- Concentrates water and nutrients in one spot
- Attracts termites that create channels for deeper infiltration

**Specifications:**
| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Diameter | 20-40 cm |
| Depth | 10-20 cm |
| Spacing | 50-80 cm apart |
| Fill | Compost/manure (1-2 handfuls) |

**Results:**
- Up to 500% yield increase in some studies
- Effective on heavily crusted soils
- Low cost, high impact

**Best For:**
- Hard, crusted soils
- Low rainfall areas (200-600mm)
- Annual crops and tree seedlings

---

### 1.2 Half-Moon Basins (Demi-Lunes)

**Origin:** Sahel region, West Africa

**Description:**
Semicircular basins (2-4 m diameter) dug perpendicular to slope, with excavated soil forming a bund on the downhill side.

**How It Works:**
- Intercepts surface runoff
- Holds water until it infiltrates
- Larger catchment than Zaï pits
- Good for trees and shrubs

**Specifications:**
| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Diameter | 2-4 m |
| Depth | 15-30 cm |
| Orientation | Perpendicular to slope |
| Spacing | 4-8 m apart |

**Results:**
- Increased infiltration significantly
- Higher tree survival rates
- Reduced erosion

**Best For:**
- Gentle slopes (1-5%)
- Tree and shrub planting
- Higher runoff areas

---

### 1.3 Swales

**Origin:** Permaculture design, adapted from various traditional systems

**Description:**
Shallow, level ditches dug along contour lines to capture and infiltrate runoff.

**How It Works:**
- Stops runoff completely
- Spreads water along the contour
- Allows slow infiltration
- Creates moist microclimate below swale

**Specifications:**
| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Width | 30-60 cm |
| Depth | 15-30 cm |
| Spacing | Every 5-20 m (depending on slope) |

**Best For:**
- Sloped land
- Larger scale water harvesting
- Creating moisture zones

---

### 1.4 Straw Checkerboards

**Origin:** China (used extensively in desert stabilization)

**Description:**
Grid patterns of straw or vegetation placed on sand surface to break wind and trap particles.

**How It Works:**
- Reduces wind speed at surface
- Traps blowing sand
- Creates micro-climate for vegetation
- Adds organic matter over time

**Specifications:**
| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Grid size | 1x1 m to 2x2 m |
| Straw height | 15-30 cm |
| Coverage | Full grid pattern |

**Best For:**
- Shifting sand dunes
- High wind areas
- Sand stabilization before planting

## 2. Water Storage & Delivery

### 2.1 Olla Irrigation

**Origin:** Ancient technique, used in China, Rome, Americas

**Description:**
Unglazed clay pots buried at root depth, filled with water that slowly seeps through the porous walls into surrounding soil.

**How It Works:**
- Water released by soil moisture tension
- Roots grow toward moisture
- No surface evaporation
- Self-regulating (wet soil = less release)

**Specifications:**
| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Material | Unglazed terracotta |
| Size | 5-20 liters |
| Burial depth | 15-40 cm (root zone) |
| Spacing | 1 olla per 2-3 plants |
| Refill frequency | 1-2x per week |

**Results:**
- 60-70% water savings vs surface irrigation
- Higher plant survival
- Deeper root systems
- Reduced weeds (dry surface)

**Best For:**
- All arid conditions
- High value plantings
- Areas with limited water

---

### 2.2 Gravity-Fed Olla Networks (Innovation)

**Origin:** Proposed integration (from research)

**Description:**
Ollas connected to water capture pits via shallow gravity channels, allowing automatic refilling during rainfall events.

**How It Works:**
1. Zaï pits and half-moons capture rainfall
2. Excess water flows through graded channels
3. Channels lead to buried ollas
4. Ollas fill and store water underground
5. Water slowly seeps to root zones

**Requirements:**
- 1-3% slope from pits to ollas
- Perforated channels or trenches
- Proper spacing and sizing

**Advantages:**
- No manual refilling needed (rain-powered)
- Captures otherwise lost runoff
- Stores water underground (no evaporation)
- Self-sustaining system

**Status:** Conceptual, needs field testing

## 3. Biological Techniques

### 3.1 Farmer Managed Natural Regeneration (FMNR)

**Origin:** Niger, developed by Tony Rinaudo

**Description:**
Protecting and pruning existing tree stumps and root systems to encourage natural regrowth, rather than planting new trees.

**How It Works:**
- Identifies living stumps/roots
- Selects strongest stems
- Prunes to encourage growth
- Protects from livestock/fire

**Results:**
- 200 million trees regenerated in Niger
- Very low cost (no planting)
- Higher survival than planted trees
- Fast results (existing root systems)

**Best For:**
- Areas with existing tree roots/stumps
- Low budget projects
- Community-managed land

---

### 3.2 Mycorrhizal Fungi Inoculation

**Origin:** Modern soil biology research

**Description:**
Adding beneficial fungi to soil to improve plant water and nutrient uptake.

**How It Works:**
- Fungi form symbiosis with plant roots
- Extend root reach for water/nutrients
- Improve drought tolerance
- Build soil structure

**Application:**
- Add to planting holes
- Mix with compost
- Apply as liquid drench

**Best For:**
- All restoration projects
- Improving plant survival
- Long-term soil health

---

### 3.3 Pioneer Vegetation Strategy

**Origin:** Ecological succession principles

**Description:**
Planting drought-tolerant, nitrogen-fixing species first to create conditions for later species.

**Typical Progression:**
1. **Pioneers** — Drought-tolerant grasses, legumes
2. **Shrubs** — Nitrogen-fixers, deep roots
3. **Trees** — Once moisture improves
4. **Climax** — Native forest/woodland

**Key Species for Arid Regions:**
| Type | Species | Benefits |
|------|---------|----------|
| Grasses | Stipa, Agropyron | Soil stabilization |
| Legumes | Acacia, Leucaena | Nitrogen fixation |
| Shrubs | Caragana, Calligonum | Windbreak, habitat |
| Trees | Elaeagnus, Populus | Long-term structure |

## 4. Soil Enhancement

### 4.1 Biochar Application

**Origin:** Amazon (terra preta), modern adaptation

**Description:**
Adding charcoal (biochar) to soil to improve water retention and nutrient holding.

**How It Works:**
- Highly porous structure holds water
- Provides habitat for microbes
- Stores carbon long-term
- Improves nutrient retention

**Application:**
- Mix with compost before adding to pits
- 5-10% by volume
- One-time application

**Best For:**
- Sandy soils
- Low organic matter soils
- Long-term improvement

---

### 4.2 Compost & Manure

**Origin:** Universal agricultural practice

**Description:**
Adding decomposed organic matter to improve soil fertility and water retention.

**Benefits:**
- Increases soil organic matter
- Feeds soil biology
- Improves structure
- Holds moisture

**Application:**
- Fill Zaï pits with mixture
- Apply as mulch
- Mix into planting holes

---

### 4.3 Mulching

**Origin:** Universal practice

**Description:**
Covering soil surface with organic material to reduce evaporation and protect biology.

**Materials:**
- Straw
- Wood chips
- Leaves
- Plant residues

**Benefits:**
- Reduces evaporation 50%+
- Moderates soil temperature
- Adds organic matter over time
- Suppresses weeds

## 5. Integrated System Design

### Optimal Combination

For fastest desert soil revival, combine:

| Priority | Technique | Purpose |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| 1 | Zaï pits + half-moons | Capture every drop of rain |
| 2 | Gravity-fed ollas | Store water underground |
| 3 | Compost + biochar | Feed soil biology |
| 4 | Pioneer vegetation | Stabilize and build |
| 5 | Mulch cover | Protect investment |

### Why This Works Faster

**Traditional approach:** Plant trees → Most die → Repeat

**Integrated approach:**
1. Build water capture → More water available
2. Store underground → Less evaporation
3. Feed soil → Biology activated
4. Plant pioneers → Higher survival
5. Protect surface → Sustained improvement

**Result:** Soil improvement in years instead of decades
