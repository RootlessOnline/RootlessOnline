## Design Philosophy

**Purpose:** A garden that supports psychedelic integration through:

1. **Grounding** — Physical connection to soil and plants
2. **Routine** — Regular activities (watering, tending, harvesting)
3. **Growth** — Witnessing plants grow mirrors personal growth
4. **Community** — Shared space with others on similar journeys
5. **Seasonality** — Connection to natural cycles

## Key Features

### 1. Keyhole Beds with Olla Irrigation

**What:** Circular raised beds with olla pots at center

**Why:**
- Water-efficient (60-70% savings)
- Low maintenance (fill weekly)
- Accessible from all angles
- Visually pleasing

**Placement:** Central feature, therapeutic area

---

### 2. Meditation/Reflection Area

**What:** Secluded bench surrounded by calming herbs

**Why:**
- Space for quiet reflection
- Sensory experience (smell, touch)
- Integration work often needs quiet space

**Plants:** Lavender, chamomile, lemon balm, mint

---

### 3. Herbal Tea Garden

**What:** Dedicated bed for tea herbs

**Why:**
- Harvestable immediately
- Connection to retreat experience (tea ceremony)
- Easy win for new gardeners

**Harvest:** Fresh herbs for tea during sessions

---

### 4. Vegetable Beds

**What:** Raised beds with easy crops

**Why:**
- Tangible results (food!)
- Multiple harvests per season
- Revenue potential

**Crops:** Tomatoes, peppers, leafy greens, culinary herbs

---

### 5. Compost Area

**What:** Simple composting system

**Why:**
- Closes the loop (regeneration)
- Educational opportunity
- Free soil amendment

## Olla Irrigation System

### What Are Ollas?

Clay pots buried in soil that slowly release water to plant roots.

### Placement

```
Top View of Bed:
        ┌───────────────┐
        │   Plant   P   │
        │   P   ◎   P   │  ◎ = Olla pot
        │   P   P   P   │  P = Plant
        │   P   ◎   P   │
        │   P   P   P   │
        └───────────────┘
```

### Spacing

- One olla per 2-3 plants
- 18-24 inches between ollas
- Fill 1-2 times per week

## Seasonal Considerations

### Spring (March-May)
- Seed starting
- Bed preparation
- First plantings
- New beginnings theme

### Summer (June-August)
- Peak growing
- Abundant harvests
- Maintenance focus
- Growth theme

### Fall (September-November)
- Final harvests
- Seed saving
- Bed preparation for winter
- Letting go theme

### Winter (December-February)
- Planning
- Indoor seed starting
- Composting
- Rest/reflection theme
